Missing Information
  • BP · DB No CTO or technical co-founder identified. For a company whose core value is a proprietary manufacturing process, this is the single biggest gap. Who will oversee the production facility build-out and scale the extrusion technology?
  • CC No customer acquisition cost (CAC) or customer lifetime value (LTV) provided. Enterprise sales to restaurant chains have long cycles — what does the cost-to-close look like? What's the average contract value and expected retention?
  • BP · CC Revenue projections jump from $3.5M to $42M in three years (12x growth) but no detailed assumptions behind this. What's the ramp model? How many accounts at what contract size?
  • DB Provisional patent status — what's the timeline to full patent grant? If the patent is denied or narrowed, how defensible is the manufacturing process? Are there trade secrets beyond the patent?
  • SQ No discussion of supply chain risks for agricultural waste inputs. Corn husks and sugarcane bagasse availability can be seasonal and regionally variable. What happens if raw material costs spike?
  • CC The plan doesn't address what happens when Eco-Products or a large plastics manufacturer decides to match on price. What's the moat beyond first-mover advantage in price parity?
Executive Summary

Greenleaf Bioplastics is a strong Series A candidate with genuine regulatory tailwinds, proven technology, and early enterprise traction. The founding team's combination of polymer science expertise and food service distribution knowledge is unusually well-matched to this opportunity. The most critical risk is the missing CTO — building a full-scale manufacturing facility without a technical leader is a serious execution concern that any investor will flag. If they fill that role with someone credible and provide detailed unit economics, this moves from a B to an A-tier opportunity.
