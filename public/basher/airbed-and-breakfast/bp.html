Thesis
The Bill Payne Scorecard Method compares a startup to the "average" funded startup in its region across seven weighted dimensions. Each dimension is scored as a percentage of average (100%), and the weighted composite produces a valuation multiplier. The philosophy: startups are relative bets — what matters isn't absolute quality but how this team, market, and product compare to the typical deal an angel sees. A score above 115% suggests above-average investment potential; below 100% means the startup underperforms the typical pitch.
Management Team 30% 95%
Three cofounders with complementary skills: two RISD designers (Joe Gebbia, Brian Chesky) and a Harvard CS engineer (Nathan Blecharcyzk) with Microsoft experience. Michael Seibel (Justin.tv) as advisor. Strong on product but no one has hospitality, marketplace operations, or travel industry experience. For a company reinventing how people find accommodation, that's a notable gap.
Size of Opportunity 25% 135%
TAM of 2B+ trips booked worldwide, SAM of 560M budget/online travelers, SOM of 84M trips (15% of SAM). The market sizing is well-sourced (Travel Industry Association, comScore) and the bottom-up revenue math ($2.1B at scale) is compelling. This is one of the largest addressable markets a startup can target.
Product / Technology 15% 120%
Live, working product with real listings across 108 cities in 17 countries. The UX flow is clean: search by city → review listings → book. Product screenshots show a thoughtful design with host profiles, pricing, and location maps. Above average for angel stage where most startups are still in prototype.
Competitive Environment 10% 110%
The competition slide maps the landscape on two axes (affordable↔expensive, offline↔online) and identifies a clear gap: no one owns the affordable + online transaction space. Couchsurfing is free but no payment, Craigslist is offline, VRBO is expensive. Positioning is smart but the gap could close quickly if incumbents adapt.
Marketing / Sales Channels 10% 115%
Three-pronged adoption strategy: events (DNC, Octoberfest, Cebit — targeting moments of hotel scarcity), partnerships (GoLoco, Kayak, Orbitz mentioned), and Craigslist dual-posting (piggybacking on 50K weekly listings). The events strategy is clever — it creates instant supply/demand imbalance. Above average creativity.
Need for Additional Funding 5% 110%
$500K angel round is modest and appropriate for a web marketplace. The ask is reasonable relative to what needs to be built. However, the lack of a use-of-funds breakdown makes it hard to assess whether $500K is actually enough.
Other (Press, IP, Partnerships) 5% 110%
Press coverage from Mashable, Webware, Josh Spear, and Springwise. User testimonials from real people with real names and cities. No patents, but for a marketplace the brand and network effects are the moat, not IP.
Weighted composite: 114% — just below the B threshold of 115%
