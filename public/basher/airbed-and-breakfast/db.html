Thesis
The Berkus Method values pre-revenue startups by assessing how much risk has been removed. Each of five categories addresses a specific existential risk. The philosophy: a startup's value at angel stage is determined not by revenue projections (which are fiction) but by how many ways the company could die and how many of those the founders have already addressed. Maximum pre-revenue valuation: $2.5M ($500K per category). This forces a conversation about what's real versus what's aspirational.
Sound Idea / Business Modeladdresses concept risk$400K
Two-sided marketplace connecting travelers with hosts, taking 10% commission. Concept is validated by existing behavior on Couchsurfing (660K users willing to stay with strangers) and Craigslist (50K temporary housing listings/week). The insight — there's a gap between free-but-risky and expensive-but-safe — is sound. Slight deduction: the business model's 10% take on $80/night bookings yields thin per-transaction revenue.
Working Prototypeaddresses technology risk$450K
Not a prototype — a live product. Website is operational at airbedandbreakfast.com with real listings across 108 cities in 17 countries. Users can search, browse profiles, and book. The product screenshots show a polished, functional UX. Technology risk is largely eliminated for v1. Deduction: trust/safety infrastructure (identity verification, payment escrow, damage protection) is not yet built.
Quality Management Teamaddresses execution risk$300K
Three cofounders with complementary skills: design (Joe + Brian, RISD) and engineering (Nathan, Harvard CS + Microsoft). Michael Seibel (Justin.tv CEO) as advisor adds startup credibility. However, no one has marketplace, hospitality, or operations experience. For a company whose core challenge is trust infrastructure and supply-side liquidity, this is a real execution risk gap.
Strategic Relationshipsaddresses market risk$250K
Partnerships mentioned with GoLoco, Kayak, and Orbitz but none appear signed — these are logos on a slide, not contracts. Press coverage from Mashable and others provides social proof. Craigslist integration is a unilateral hack, not a partnership. The Seibel advisor relationship is real. Market risk is partially but not substantially mitigated.
Product Rollout / Salesaddresses production risk$300K
The product is live with real users and bookings. User testimonials from DC, Atlanta, Austin, and Ontario confirm cross-geography usage. The events-based adoption strategy (DNC) proved the concept works at point of demand. However, ongoing traction metrics aren't shown — we don't know booking volume, growth rate, or repeat usage. Early signal is positive but thin.
Total: $1.7M / $2.5M (68%)
