Thesis
Sequoia's "Elements of Enduring Companies" is a qualitative gate, not a scorecard. The philosophy: before analyzing financials or traction, ask whether this company has the fundamental characteristics of businesses that last decades. These six elements are pattern-matched from Sequoia's portfolio of companies like Apple, Google, and Cisco. Each element is binary — the company either exhibits it or doesn't. This is the filter that determines whether a deeper conversation is warranted.
Clarity of PurposePass
"Book rooms with locals, rather than hotels." Seven words. Passes the business-card test perfectly. Anyone who reads that sentence understands what the company does, who it serves, and how it's different. This is the gold standard for clarity — the Sequoia pitch template literally asks "can you describe your company in one sentence?" and this is the answer.
Large MarketPass
2B+ trips booked worldwide (TAM), 560M budget/online travelers (SAM). The travel accommodation market is one of the largest consumer markets on Earth. Even the narrow initial wedge — events where hotels sell out — taps into a multi-billion dollar annual flow. The $2.1B revenue projection at 15% SAM capture is ambitious but the market can support it. This is comfortably above Sequoia's $1B threshold.
Rich CustomersFail
This is the one miss. AB&B targets budget travelers — people choosing it specifically because it's cheaper than hotels. These aren't customers who'll pay a premium; they're customers who'll leave for a cheaper option. Hosts are also price-sensitive — they're monetizing spare space, not running a premium hospitality business. Neither side of the marketplace fits the "rich customers who move fast and pay a premium" test.
FocusPass
One product, one value proposition: connect travelers with local hosts for affordable, authentic accommodation. The deck doesn't try to be a travel agency, a tour operator, or an experience platform (that comes later). In 2008, this is a focused, single-purpose marketplace. The three benefits (save money, make money, share culture) all serve the same core transaction.
Pain KillersPass
The problem slide nails it: price is a major concern, hotels disconnect you from the city, and no easy way exists to book a room with a local. When hotels sell out for events (DNC, Octoberfest, Cebit), the pain is acute — people literally can't find accommodation. This isn't a vitamin; it's a painkiller for travelers who are priced out or sold out. The regulatory and market timing makes this increasingly painful as hotel prices rise.
Contrarian ThinkingPass
"Would you let a stranger sleep in your home?" In 2008, this was genuinely contrarian — most people and most investors thought it was crazy. Every VC on Sand Hill Road rejected this idea. The conventional wisdom said trust couldn't be established between strangers online for something as intimate as accommodation. AB&B bet that it could, and that bet challenged the entire hotel industry's assumption that you need a trusted brand to feel safe sleeping somewhere new.
5 / 6 elements pass
