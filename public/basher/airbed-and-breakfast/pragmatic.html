P1Path to first paying customerPass
Already have real users and bookings. Launched at 2008 DNC in Denver, Craigslist dual-posting strategy for ongoing supply, and events-based demand generation targeting conferences and festivals.
P2Rate limiter to first $10MFail
Not addressed. The obvious bottleneck is supply-side liquidity — getting enough quality hosts in enough cities to make the marketplace useful. The chicken-and-egg marketplace cold start problem isn't discussed.
P3Operating cash required (3/6/12mo)Fail
No monthly or quarterly cash burn provided. The deck says "$500K angel round" and "12 months financing" but doesn't show what that $500K covers month by month.
P4Capital expenditure requiredPass
Pure web platform — CapEx is legitimately minimal (hosting, payment processing setup). Appropriate for the business type.
P5Survival runway without new fundingFail
$500K with no stated burn rate makes runway incalculable. "12 months financing" implies 12 months of runway but the math isn't shown. At the 10% take rate on early-stage bookings, revenue won't sustain the company.
P6First critical hireFail
Three cofounders covering design and engineering, but no discussion of who else is needed. A marketplace needs trust/safety, customer support, and growth — all unaddressed.
P7Unit economics todayIncomplete
Average room price ($80/night), average fee ($25), and take rate (10%) are stated. But no CAC, no cost-to-serve per booking, and no data on how many bookings are actually happening per month.
P8Single point of failureFail
Not addressed. The obvious SPOF is trust — one bad incident (theft, assault, property damage) could kill the brand before it scales. No discussion of identity verification, insurance, or dispute resolution.
P9Regulatory or legal gatePass
In 2008, short-term rental regulation was minimal. No licensing or zoning issues identified at this stage. The regulatory storm comes later, but the deck is accurate for its time.
P10Revenue concentration riskPass
Marketplace model distributes revenue across many hosts and guests by design. No single customer or city dependence at this stage.
P11Founder proof of executionPass
Nathan built Facebook apps with 75,000 users and worked at Microsoft. Joe holds a patent (CritBuns). Brian ran a design consultancy. Together they built and launched a working product, got press from Mashable and others, and landed real users and testimonials. Michael Seibel (Justin.tv) as advisor adds credibility.
